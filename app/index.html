Who I Be

Writer. Traveler. Sportsman. Dreamer. Mistake-Maker. Lesson-Learner. Bounce-Backer. Hustle+Hacker.

Ever since I made my first website (NHRT*) in high school and witnessed it take the student body by storm, I've been hooked by the power of the web. Upon graduating from the University of Texas at Austin, though, I tried my hand at a few different jobs and continued to shape my mind and character through extensive travel. Although I continued to dabble in web development through freelance projects, I came full circle in 2014 when I decided (from a cramped hostel bunk bed in Lima, Peru) it was time to take my web skills to the next level and enroll in an intensive 12-week "code school" back in the States.

*Please see the "Fun Facts" section below for more info.

I believe the depth of my exposure to people from different walks of life and corners of the globe uniquely positions me to tailor web ventures in ways that will ultimately catch on. But the bottom line is simple: the key is to believe in what we're working on and the effect we can have on others. Otherwise it's simply not worth it.

Majestic Thai

Click to see Majestic Thai

When my instructor assigned this homework project over a long weekend, I was ecstatic. After all, I lived in Thailand and it always pains me to pay $15 at a Thai joint in America for a dish I used to eat on the street for less than a dollar. So don't be alarmed when you notice all the prices are listed in Thai Baht, as I promise the food is up to par and - Thai fare or not - now I'm ready to help any restaurant accept orders from their website!
